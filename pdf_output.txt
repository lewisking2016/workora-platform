1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY 
JuaKazi is a digital Trust Passport for Africa's invisible workforce—the 80% of 
workers who operate in the informal economy, unbanked, unverifiable, and unknown 
to the formal hiring, credit, and insurance markets. 
We are building a unified mobile ecosystem (video, chat, professional profile, and 
shareable trust card) that gives every informal worker a permanent, portable, verifiable 
reputation. They find work instantly. They access loans and insurance. They build a 
career. We generate revenue through transaction fees, subscriptions, and licensing trust 
data to financial institutions. And 20% of all platform revenue is automatically routed 
into a Bridge Fund that funds apprenticeships and digital skills training for Kenya's 
most vulnerable. 
At 1 million users, JuaKazi projects gross monthly revenue of KES 135 million 
(~USD 1 million), with a net operating revenue of KES 108 million after mission 
allocation. 
We are seeking seed investment and strategic partners to launch the pilot and scale 
nation-wide.

3. THE SOLUTION: JUA KAZI 
JuaKazi is a single, unified mobile application with four key surfaces that share one 
identity, one trust graph, and one reputation. 
Surface Description 
JuaKazi 
Feed 
A short-form vertical video feed (like Instagram/TikTok) where workers post 
proof-of-work content. Every video is tied to a verified completed gig, turning 
content into unfakeable social proof. 
JuaKazi 
Chat 
Integrated directly with WhatsApp Business API. Workers receive messages on 
their existing WhatsApp. In-app fallback available. All communication 
metadata feeds the trust score. 
JuaKazi 
Profile 
A rich, LinkedIn-like professional profile—but built on verified work history, 
not school certificates. Trust score, badges, voice testimonials, gig timeline, and 
portable digital credentials. 
JuaKazi 
Trust Card 
A lightweight, shareable web link (jua.kazi/@username) that displays essential 
trust data. Works on any phone via SMS or WhatsApp—no app download 
needed. The bridge to the offline world. 
One app. One trust graph. Multiple surfaces.

4. THE ECOSYSTEM & USER JOURNEY 
1. A worker (e.g., a plumber) downloads the app and creates a profile in 30 
seconds: photo, voice intro, skill selection. 
2. They post a 30-second video of themselves fixing a pipe. The video gets the "膆  
Verified Gig" badge once it's tied to a paid, rated job. 
3. A hirer nearby searches "plumber" and sees the worker's Feed video, profile, and 
4.8-star rating. They message via WhatsApp directly from the app. 
4. The gig is agreed upon, payment held in M-Pesa escrow. Upon completion, both 
parties rate each other. The worker's trust score updates. 
5. The worker shares their Trust Card link via SMS to another potential client. The 
client opens it on a feature phone, sees the rating and voice testimonials, and 
hires. 
6. After 20 completed gigs, the worker qualifies for a KES 5,000 fuel loan, offered 
through a partner MFI, based solely on their JuaKazi trust data. 
7. A corporate sponsor funds apprenticeships through the Uwezo Fund. A youth 
without skills learns under a 4.9-rated master craftsperson. Progress is tracked 
and visible to the donor.

6. MISSION & SOCIAL IMPACT: THE IMEANTECH BRIDGE FUND 
20% of all platform revenue is automatically routed into the ImeanTech Bridge Fund—
a transparent, auditable social impact engine. 
Fund Allocation: 
 Community Agents: Stipends for ImeanTech-trained youth who onboard 
workers, verify IDs, and facilitate apprenticeships in underserved areas. 
 Apprenticeship Sponsorships: Pays master craftspersons to train unskilled 
youth, tracked transparently. 
 Digital Literacy Bursaries: Scholarships for underprivileged Kenyans to gain 
tech skills. 
 Worker Emergency Relief: Support for high-rated workers facing sudden crises. 
This is not a CSR afterthought. It's a core differentiator that builds community loyalty, 
attracts impact investors, and positions JuaKazi as a national digital public good 
aligned with Kenya's Vision 2030 and digital inclusion goals.
